Cancellation Rights & Distance Selling

Because Epic Moments accepts event bookings "at a distance" (over the phone or via email), our clients are protected by the UK Consumer Contracts Regulations[cite: 5].

The 14-Day Cooling-Off Period

Clients have the legal right to cancel their contract within 14 days of making the booking to receive a full refund of their deposit[cite: 10, 11]. However, this standard rule only applies if the event is more than 14 days away and no custom items have been ordered[cite: 12].

Bespoke Goods Exemption

The 14-day cooling-off period does not apply to any materials made to your exact specifications or clearly personalized items (e.g., custom-printed welcome boards or non-returnable specific balloon colors)[cite: 15, 16]. The client is entirely responsible for the cost of these goods, and we will deduct the exact cost of these custom purchases from any deposit refund[cite: 17, 18].

Short-Notice Bookings

For events taking place in less than 14 days from the date of booking, paying the deposit acts as an explicit agreement for us to begin work immediately[cite: 20, 21]. If the client cancels before the event, they lose the right to a full refund. Epic Moments will legally retain a portion of the deposit to cover reasonable costs incurred up to the exact moment of cancellation[cite: 22, 23]. Once event setup is fully complete, the client completely loses their legal right to cancel the contract[cite: 26].

How to Cancel

To exercise your right to cancel, you must send an email to info@epicmoments.co.uk stating your name, event date, and a clear declaration of cancellation[cite: 29, 31]. Cancellations will not be accepted via verbal phone calls or casual text messages[cite: 30]. Valid refunds will be issued to the original payment method within 14 days of receiving the official notice[cite: 34, 35, 36].

Legal, Health, and Safety

Epic Moments prioritizes safety to protect our clients, their guests, and our business operations[cite: 43].

Insurance & Structural Safety

We maintain comprehensive Public Liability Insurance[cite: 46]. Every freestanding structure we install is heavily weighted at the base to prevent accidents[cite: 53]. If an Event Manager deems a venue space unsafe for a specific setup (e.g., high winds or uneven flooring), they possess the legal authority to halt the setup or alter the design, and the client will not be entitled to a refund[cite: 55, 56].

Limitation of Liability

Epic Moments takes no legal or financial responsibility for the actions or failures of third-party vendors[cite: 50]. Furthermore, we are not liable for any injuries, damages, or breakages caused by guests interacting improperly with the decorations[cite: 64]. By paying the deposit, the client agrees to indemnify and hold Epic Moments harmless against claims arising from guest misuse of our hired equipment[cite: 65].

Force Majeure

We cannot be held financially responsible for failing to deliver services due to extreme events beyond our control, such as acts of God, extreme weather, or government lockdowns[cite: 67, 68]. In these situations, we will not issue cash refunds for purchased materials, but will apply the existing payments as a credit toward a rescheduled event date[cite: 69, 70].

UK GDPR & Data Protection Policy

We collect the exact information necessary to provide our services and manage our business accounts, including Identity, Contact, Event, Financial, and Communication data[cite: 148, 149, 150, 151, 152, 153].

How We Use and Share Your Data

We collect this data to fulfill our event contracts, comply with HMRC legal obligations, and for legitimate business interests[cite: 157, 158, 160]. All digital records are stored securely, and we absolutely do not sell, rent, or trade client information to third parties[cite: 163, 165]. We only share data with necessary parties, such as venue coordinators for access, accountants for tax reporting, or legal authorities if required by law[cite: 168, 169, 170].

Data Retention and Your Legal Rights

General inquiries are deleted within 12 months, but by UK law, we must keep all financial records and contracts for 7 years[cite: 173, 174]. Under UK GDPR, you have the right to access the data we hold about you, request corrections, or ask for erasure (excluding data legally required for HMRC tax records)[cite: 177, 178, 179, 180]. If you feel your data has been mishandled, you may complain directly to us or to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO)[cite: 183, 184].
